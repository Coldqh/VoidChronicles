Void Chronicles v0.28.0 — Simulation Stability

Purpose
-------
Hardens the autonomous simulation for long campaigns, damaged legacy states and large galaxies.

Core changes
------------
- Replaces the old 1,000-event truncation with centralized bounded retention.
- Keeps the latest event-sourced state snapshot for every live entity.
- Preserves era transitions, regressions, collapses, wars, peace, occupations and player consequences.
- Repairs duplicate event ids and bidirectional causal links.
- Normalizes NaN, infinity, negative populations/resources and out-of-range 0–100 metrics.
- Removes or repairs orphaned settlements, population groups and trade routes.
- Reconciles settlement population with population-group totals.
- Deduplicates and caps the scheduled queue at 25,000 records.
- Repairs nextSequence after imported or old saves.
- Resolves wars that are exhausted, idle for 20 years or active for more than 120 years.
- Limits war updates to once per 30 days.
- Adds centralized cooldowns and compaction for revolts, riots, strikes, reforms and demographic decline.

Kernel integration
------------------
After extracting the archive over the repository, run:

  node APPLY-v0.28.mjs

The installer patches the current src/simulation/kernel.ts in place. It is idempotent and safe to run more than once.

Compatibility
-------------
- Base release: v0.27.0.
- APP_VERSION: 0.28.0.
- APP_CODENAME: SIMULATION_STABILITY.
- SAVE_SCHEMA_VERSION remains 13.
- No save migration is required.

Validation
----------
- Strict TypeScript validation of all changed simulation modules.
- Strict JSX/type validation of Chronicle and Living World integration.
- Existing v0.18, v0.21, v0.24 and v0.27 deterministic suites.
- Living-history transition regression: medieval -> gunpowder.
- Living-history collapse regression: atomic -> modern.
- Nine new stability scenarios.
- Kernel history retention beyond 1,000 events.
- Histories representing 100, 1,000 and 10,000 simulated years.
- Valid 1,500-system world state.
- ZIP extraction and byte-for-byte verification.
